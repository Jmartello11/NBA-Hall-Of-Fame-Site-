Philadelphia 76ers

Iverson attented Gerogetown to play his college ball. After two successful seasons there he would declare for the draft in 1996. Iverson was selcted first overall by the Philadelphia 76ers. Offically listed at 6ft Iverson became the shortest first overll pick ever. In his first season with them he crossed up Michael Jordan, and beat Wilt Chamberlains rookie record of 3 straight games with 40 points, accomplishing it in five. He would finish the year averaging 23.5 points, 7.5 assists and 2.1 steals per game. He was named rookie of the year. Iverson would contiune to help imporove the 76ers. In the shortened season of 1998 Iverson would lead the leaguie with 26.8 points, and would make it to the playoffs. Ultimatley losing in the seond round to the Pacers.

The next year the 76ers would make the playoffs with a 49-33 record. In the playoffs Iverson would average 26.2 points, 4.8 assists, and 1.3 steals. Howevert they were eliminated in the second round to the Pacers for the second year in a row. He would be selected to the allstar game for the first time, which would start an eleven year streak. In 200 the 76ers tried to trade Iverson because of a fued with head coach Larry Brown. However they would make up and out aside there differences. In the 2000-2001 season Iverson would have his best year. The 76ers started out 10-0, and he would go on to win the allstar game MVP. The 76ers finished with a 56-26 record earining the number 1 seed. He averaged 31.1 points per game that year, leading the league. He also got the steals title with 2.5 per game. Iverson won the MVP becoming the shortest and lightest player to ever win the award. They would later advance to the Finals against the defending champions the Lakers. The 76ers won the first game where Iverson dropped 48points and infamously stepped over Tyronn Lue. They would lose the next four games to the Lakers. Iverson wore an arm sleeve to combat bursitis in his right arm. Players like Kobe and Carmelo would adopt the sleeve as a fashion statment.

The next season Iverson would get injured but still average 31.4 points. They would make the playoffs, but lose to the Celtics. Iversons relationship with Brown would be strained when Brown crtizied Iverson over missing practice. In 2002-2003 Iverson averaged 27.6 points and would make the playoffs with a 48-34 record. The would lose in the second round to the Pistons. Brown would leave in 2003 and Randy Ayers would become head coach. In 2003 the 76ers were irrelevant and bounced back in 2004 under the new head coach Jim OBrien. Iverson would average 31 points and 8 assists to win another socring title and make it to the playoffs again, where they would lose to the Pistons lead by L:arry Brown. In the 2005-2006 season, Iverson averaged a career high 33 points but the 76ers would miss the playoffs again.

In 2006 it was rumored that Iverson was going to Denver. After a terrible start to the 2006 season he demanded a trade. Iverson ended his years with the 76ers after 10 seasons with an average of 28.1 points and was second all time on their list for points with 19,583. They would not win another playoff sereis after his depature until 2012.
